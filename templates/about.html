Project Overview

The primary objective of this project is to design and develop a scalable and reliable Spoken Keyword Spotting (KWS) system. In addition, the project seeks to deploy a KWS-based healthcare information dissemination platform that supports multiple Indian languages, including Assamese, Bangla, Bodo, Manipuri, Hindi, Indian English, Mizo, Nagamese, and Nepali. A further objective of this initiative is the development of a versatile speech corpus for three North-East Indian languages—Mizo, Nepali, and Nagamese—to facilitate research and advancement in speech and language technologies.

Deliverables

  • API for the Spoken Keyword Spotting (KWS) System
  • Localization of Health-related Information in seven languages: Assamese, Bangla, Bodo, Manipuri, Mizo, Nagamese, and Nepali
  • Speech-based Health Information Dissemination System (HiDS)
  • Speech Corpora Development — 150 hours each for Mizo, Nagamese, and Nepali
  • Data Curation — Hindi and Indian English data from the Bhashini Project
  • Development of KWS System for Hindi and Indian English
